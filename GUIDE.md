## 1. For Coaches — Quick Start

As a coach you can:

- Browse and watch animated plays in the **public library**
- **Create your own plays** in the editor and save them
- **Publish plays** to your team's playbook
- **Share a link** with your squad — no app download, no login needed for players

---

## 2. Signing In

Playbook uses **magic links** — no password needed.

1. Click **Sign in** in the top-right corner of any page
2. Enter your email address
3. Click **Send Magic Link**
4. Open your email and click the link — you're in

Your session stays active, so you won't need to sign in again on the same device.

> **Players never need to sign in.** They just tap the link you share.

---

## 3. Browsing the Play Library

Go to **`/moves`** (the home page) to see the public play library.

**Filtering:**
- Use the **Level** chips to filter by Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced
- Use the **Type** chips to filter by Attack Structure / Set Piece / Defence / Kick Game / Restarts
- Use the **search bar** to find plays by name
- Combine filters — e.g. "Beginner + Set Piece"

**Clicking a play card** opens the full play viewer.

## 4. Watching a Play

Each play has two viewing modes, selectable at the top of the canvas.

### Overview Mode (auto-play)

- The play **animates automatically** — players move, lines draw in
- Use **▶ / ⏸** to play and pause
- Use the **0.5× / 1× / 2×** speed buttons to change playback speed
- The play loops back to the start when finished

### Step by Step Mode (manual)

- Use **← →** arrows (or the step dots) to advance one step at a time
- Each step shows what happens at that moment in the play
- **Option cards** appear below the canvas — click to toggle individual options on/off
  - Option 1 = Yellow
  - Option 2 = Blue
  - Option 3 = Orange
- Tap any option card to isolate that line, or show all at once

**Reading a step:**
- **Grey circles** = attack players (with jersey number)
- **Red circles** = defence players
- **Yellow oval** = ball carrier
- **Solid arrow lines** = run
- **Dashed arrow lines** = pass

### Info Panel

Below the canvas (or in the right column on desktop) you'll find:
- **What is it** — tactical overview
- **When to use it** — game situations
- **Why it works** — the coaching rationale
- **Key positions** — who does what
- **Options & alternatives** — variations
- **Common mistakes** — what to watch for

## 5. Creating a Play

Click **Create Play** in the navbar (or go to `/editor`).

### Step 1 — Choose a template

When the editor opens, the **Templates panel** appears. Pick a starting point:

| Template | Use for |
|----------|---------|
| Scrum Left / Centre / Right | Scrum plays |
| Lineout Left / Right | Lineout plays |
| Kickoff | Restart plays |
| 22m Drop | Drop-out plays |
| Blank | Start from scratch |

### Step 2 — Select a field zone

Use the **Zone selector** in the control strip to set which part of the field the play takes place on. This changes the shape and aspect ratio of the canvas.

### Step 3 — Draw your play

Use the toolbar on the left (desktop) or top (mobile):

| Tool | What it does |
|------|-------------|
| **Select** | Move players around by dragging |
| **Run** | Click a player, then click a destination to draw a run line |
| **Pass** | Click the passer, then click the receiver |
| **Arrow** | Draw a free-form directional arrow |
| **Circle** | Draw a highlight circle |
| **Text** | Place a text label |
| **Target** | Place a target/zone marker |
| **Eraser** | Click any element to delete it |

**Colour-coding options:**  
Use the **Option selector** (1 / 2 / 3) in the control strip before drawing a line. Lines drawn in Option 1 are yellow, Option 2 are blue, Option 3 are orange.

### Step 4 — Add steps

A play can have multiple steps (like slides in a presentation).

- Click **+ Step** in the step tab bar at the bottom to add a new step
- Each step is a snapshot — players in their positions at that moment
- Steps animate sequentially in the viewer

### Step 5 — Add play info (optional)

Click the **Info** button in the control strip to open the play info panel. Fill in:
- Title, difficulty, category
- What is it / When to use / Why it works / Key positions / Common mistakes

## 6. Saving and Sharing a Play

### Save

Click **Save** in the editor header. The play is saved to your **My Plays** page.

> First save requires you to be signed in. If you're not signed in yet, a sign-in prompt appears.

### Share a direct link

Once saved, click **Share** in the editor header (or the share button on the viewer page). The link is copied to your clipboard. Anyone with the link can watch the play — no sign-in needed.

### Use a library play

On any library or team play, click **Use this play**. A copy is saved to your My Plays so you can edit it. If you're not signed in, a prompt appears first.

## 7. Setting Up Your Team

1. Sign in, then click **Team** in the navbar (or go to `/team`)
2. First visit: enter your **team name** (e.g. "Belsize Park RFC") and click **Create Team**
3. Your team dashboard opens — you'll see your team name, a team link, and an empty playbook

---

## 8. Publishing Plays to Your Team

From the **Team Dashboard** (`/team`):

1. Click **+ Add Play**
2. A panel shows all your saved plays (from My Plays)
3. Click any play to add it to your team playbook — it's published immediately
4. The play appears in the team playbook list

**Reordering plays:**
- Use the **↑ ↓ arrows** on each row to change the order players will see them

**Removing a play:**
- Click **Remove** on any row to take it off the team playbook
- The play still exists in your My Plays — only the team listing is removed
- The play's direct link still works for anyone who has it

**Renaming your team:**
- Edit the team name in the **Team Settings** section at the bottom of the dashboard and click **Save**

---

## 9. Sharing the Team Link

1. On the Team Dashboard, click **Team Link ↗**
2. The link is copied to your clipboard
3. Paste it into your **WhatsApp group, email, or training platform**

The link goes to your team playbook page — e.g. `https://playbook.app/team/belsize-park-rfc-a3b2c1`

Players tap the link and see all your published plays immediately. No account, no download.

## 10. For Players — Quick Start

Your coach will share either:
- A **team link** — e.g. `playbook.app/team/your-team-name`
- A **direct play link** — e.g. `playbook.app/moves/out-the-back-option-2847`

**You don't need an account.** Just tap the link.

### On the team page

- You'll see a grid of your team's plays
- Tap any play card to watch it

### Watching a play

- **Overview** tab — the play animates automatically. Watch it once to get the picture.
- **Step by Step** tab — tap through each phase manually. Use this to study individual moments.
- Use **0.5×** speed if it moves too fast
- Tap the **option cards** at the bottom to isolate specific lines if there are multiple options

### On mobile

The viewer is designed for phones:
- Everything stacks vertically
- All buttons are large enough to tap with a thumb
- Works in any browser — no app download needed
